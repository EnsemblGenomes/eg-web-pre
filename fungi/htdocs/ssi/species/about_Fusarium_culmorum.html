About the Fusarium culmorum genome

The ascomycete fungus Fusarium culmorum is one of the most commonly found pathogenic species identified on flowering wheat spikes that cause yield losses and mycotoxin contamination of grain in the United Kingdom.

Assembly and annotation

Genomic DNA of F. culmorum strain UK99 was sequenced on a 454 GS FLXÏ© (Roche Diagnostics Corporation) at the University of Liverpool. The draft assembly is 41,928,875 bp in length was assembled to produce 223 scaffolds, which were further scaffolded using alignments to the closely related Fusarium graminearum strain PH-1. Annotation was performed via Maker 2.
